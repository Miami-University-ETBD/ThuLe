Vietnamese Dishes

Dish Descriptions

  • helpPho
    Pho is a beloved Vietnamese noodle soup, characterized by its fragrant and flavorful broth, typically simmered for hours with aromatic spices and beef bones. This savory base is then poured over soft rice noodles and thinly sliced meat, often beef, which cooks gently in the hot broth. Garnished with fresh herbs like cilantro, mint, and basil, along with bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili peppers, pho offers a customizable and deeply satisfying culinary experience that is considered Vietnam's national dish.
  • helpMi Quang
    Mi Quang is a vibrant and flavorful noodle dish hailing from the Quang Nam province in central Vietnam. Broad, turmeric-infused rice noodles form the base, topped with a rich, often scant, broth that is intensely flavored with shrimp, pork, and sometimes chicken. Peanuts, crispy rice crackers (banh trang me), fresh herbs like mint and basil, and a squeeze of lime are essential garnishes, contributing to the dish's unique textures and a delightful interplay of savory, sweet, and nutty notes.
  • helpBun Bo Hue
    Bun Bo Hue is a robust and spicy Vietnamese noodle soup originating from the city of Hue. Its flavorful broth, simmered with beef bones, lemongrass, and fermented shrimp paste, distinguishes it from pho with a bolder and more intense taste. Served with thick rice vermicelli noodles, slices of beef shank, pork knuckles, and sometimes congealed pig's blood, Bun Bo Hue is typically garnished with fresh herbs like mint and cilantro, along with banana blossoms and lime wedges, offering a complex and deeply satisfying culinary experience.
  • helpBun Bo Hue
    Banh Mi is a vibrant Vietnamese sandwich typically served on a toasted baguette with a thin, crisp crust and soft interior. It's filled with a savory combination of meats, such as grilled pork, pate, or cold cuts, along with pickled vegetables like carrots and daikon, fresh cilantro, cucumber slices, and often a smear of spicy mayonnaise or chili sauce, creating a delightful balance of textures and flavors.
  • helpBanh Cuon
    Banh Cuon is a delicate Vietnamese dish featuring thin, steamed rice flour rolls filled with a savory mixture of ground pork and wood ear mushrooms. These silky and slightly chewy rolls are typically topped with crispy fried shallots and often served alongside Vietnamese pork sausage (cha lua), fresh bean sprouts, and a flavorful dipping sauce called nuoc cham, making for a light yet satisfying meal or snack.
